3D CULL




   Culling is the process in 3D-rendering that removes any piece of the output object that does not contribute to the     final image.

   Culling checks the faces that are front facing towards the viewer and renders those faces and discards the remaining    faces off the object.

   There are various methods for culling such as frustum culling, cell-based occlusion culling, etc. But we are using    back-face culling because it reduces the amount of rendering it has to do for showing the visible planes of a 3D object.

   In order to implement back-face culling, we calculate the dot product of the surface normal and the camera-to-    triangle vector.

   If the dot product is greater than or equal to zero, the respective polygon or triangle is to be discarded where P is the    view point, V0 is the first vertex of a triangle and N is its normal, defined as a cross product of two vectors representing    sides of the triangle adjacent to V0.

   Since the cross product is non-commutative, defining the normal in terms of cross product allows to specify normal    direction relative to triangle surface using vertex order.
    If points are already in view space, P can be assumed to be (0, 0, 0), the origin.
